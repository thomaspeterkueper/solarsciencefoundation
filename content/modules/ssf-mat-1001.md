# Numbers and Patterns

Everything in trade and building starts with counting. Before you can plan a route or judge a profit, you need to be sure about three things: how many, how much more, and how much is left.

## Counting and comparing

Counting tells you *how many*. Comparing tells you which amount is more, which is less, or whether two amounts are equal. "More", "less" and "equal" are the first patterns behind every later calculation.

## Addition and subtraction

Addition (+) puts amounts together: 3 crates + 4 crates = 7 crates.

Subtraction (−) takes an amount away, or finds the difference between two amounts: 7 crates − 4 crates = 3 crates.

Profit is just a subtraction: what you get back, minus what you paid.

## Worked example — a trade run

You buy 5 units of ore for 8 credits each and sell all 5 for 13 credits each.

- Cost: five equal amounts added together, 8 + 8 + 8 + 8 + 8 = 40 credits (this is 5 × 8).
- Revenue: 13 + 13 + 13 + 13 + 13 = 65 credits (5 × 13).
- Profit: 65 − 40 = 25 credits.

So the run earns **25 credits**. If the sell price fell to 8 credits, profit would be 40 − 40 = 0 — you would break even, earning nothing.

## Learning goals

- Count and compare amounts.
- Add and subtract whole numbers.
- Read profit as revenue minus cost.
